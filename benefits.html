Benefits

There are a lot of benefits that come from crocheting. Doing something you enjoy can release dopamine and serotonin in the brain. Craft activities have shown to increase happiness levels and self-esteem. I have definitely experienced this after completing projects that took hours or days of work. Crochet can also be used to relieve anxiety and stress. Some people may find it relaxing to do repetitive motions and counting, letting them put their worries to the side. This peaceful mindset can also help with sleep issues. Spending some time crocheting can soothe the mind and body to eventually promote sleep. And according to a study published in the American Journal of Psychiatry, crocheting is a good way to stimulate your brain. By learning and using crochet techniques, stitches, patterns, and reading, we can reduce the risk of Alzheimer's disease by 30-50%.
